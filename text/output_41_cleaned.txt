MAGNETIC RESONANCE IMAGING (MRI) BRAIN 7/14/2023 8:00 AM INDICATION: 58-year-old female with worsening headaches and history of cerebral aneurysm. COMPARISON: MRI dated 5/20/2022. METHOD: MRI scans were completed using a 3 Tesla machine at Regional Medical Center. No complications were encountered during the procedure.
FINDINGS: The MRI reveals a 3mm aneurysm on the anterior communicating artery, unchanged since the last scan. There are no signs of rupture or significant growth. No new infarcts, masses, or abnormal enhancements were observed. Ventricles and sulci are within normal limits for the patient's age.
IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. Stable anterior communicating artery aneurysm without change compared to prior imaging. 2. No acute intracranial pathology detected. Counseled patient on lifestyle modifications and scheduled a follow-up for ongoing monitoring.
