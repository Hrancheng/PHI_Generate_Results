MR ABDOMEN WITHOUT CONTRAST, 03/15/2023 10:30 AM INDICATION: 54-year-old male with history of chronic liver disease and recent unexplained weight loss. COMPARISON: MRI dated 11/28/2022. TECHNIQUE: High-resolution MR imaging of the abdomen was performed without the use of IV contrast. FINDINGS: The liver is enlarged and demonstrates diffuse heterogeneity suggestive of cirrhosis and fibrosis. No focal liver lesions are observed. The spleen is mildly enlarged measuring 12 cm. Portal and splenic vein calibers are within normal limits. There is no evidence of ascites. The pancreas, adrenal glands, and kidneys appear normal in size and morphology without any focal lesions. The gallbladder is normal. No biliary ductal dilatation. IMPRESSION: 1. Features consistent with cirrhosis without any evidence of focal hepatic lesions. 2. Mild splenomegaly. No other abnormal findings noted in the abdomen.
