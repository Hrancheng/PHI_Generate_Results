Example 21:
ULTRASOUND OF THE ABDOMEN 10/23/2022 CLINICAL INDICATION: Right upper quadrant pain. HISTORY: Previous episodes of cholecystitis. COMPARISON: Ultrasound dated 6/12/2020 IMPRESSION: FINDINGS: Examination of the gallbladder shows multiple gallstones with the largest stone measuring approximately 1.0 cm. There is also mild gallbladder wall thickening indicative of inflammation but no definitive evidence of cholecystitis. The common bile duct is normal in diameter. The liver exhibits normal echogenicity with no focal liver lesions. The pancreas and spleen are unremarkable. The kidneys are normal in size with no evidence of hydronephrosis or stones. IMPRESSION: 1. Multiple gallstones with mild gallbladder wall thickening but no definite acute cholecystitis. 2. Normal liver, pancreas, spleen, and kidneys.
