ABDOMINAL CT SCAN 4/11/2022 INDICATION: 72-year-old male with complaints of chronic abdominal pain and recent weight loss. TECHNIQUE: Helical CT was performed using 100 mL of intravenous contrast, covering the abdomen from diaphragm to pelvis. COMPARISON: Previous CT scan dated 3/10/2020. FINDINGS: The liver appears enlarged with multiple low attenuation areas suggestive of metastatic disease. The pancreas is atrophic without ductal dilatation or mass. The adrenal glands are unremarkable. Kidneys show age-appropriate changes with no evidence of masses or stones. Aortoiliac calcifications noted without significant stenosis. Mild splenomegaly observed. The stomach and intestines are unremarkable. There is no free fluid or lymphadenopathy. IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. Hepatomegaly with suspicious lesions likely representing metastases. 2. Pancreatic atrophy without specific pathology. 3. Mild splenomegaly. Further evaluation with a PET scan is recommended to assess for malignancy.
