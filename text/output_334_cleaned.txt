Example 21:
MRI BRAIN WITH AND WITHOUT CONTRAST, dated 10/25/2022 at 8:30 AM. CLINICAL INDICATION: 58-year-old female with a history of chronic headaches and recent onset of visual disturbances. COMPARISON: Previous MRI dated 9/20/2021. TECHNIQUE: MRI was conducted using a 3 Tesla magnet with multiplanar, multisequence imaging including diffusion-weighted imaging and gadolinium-based contrast enhancement. FINDINGS: There is a well-defined enhancing lesion measuring 2 cm x 1.5 cm at the right occipital lobe, suggestive of a meningioma. No evidence of edema or mass effect. The ventricles and sulci are normal in size. No acute infarct or hemorrhage is noted. The pituitary and pineal glands appear normal. Visualized cranial nerves are unremarkable. IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. New finding of a right occipital lobe meningioma without secondary effects. 2. No other significant intracranial abnormalities. Follow up with neurosurgery is recommended.
