Example 21:
CT ABDOMEN AND PELVIS WITH CONTRAST 07/25/2021. 
CLINICAL INDICATION: Evaluate for abdominal pain and known pancreatic mass.
TECHNIQUE: Helical CT performed from diaphragm to pubic symphysis with 100 mL of IV contrast.
FINDINGS:
- Pancreas: Previously noted 3 cm mass in the head of the pancreas, currently measures 3.2 cm. No new lesions.
- Liver: No hepatic lesions or biliary dilation.
- Gallbladder: Normal in appearance, no stones.
- Spleen: Unremarkable.
- Kidneys: Normal size, contour, and enhancement. No hydronephrosis or stones.
- Aorta and Major Branches: Unremarkable.
- Gastrointestinal Tract: No bowel obstruction or focal colonic lesion.
- Adrenal Glands: Normal.
- Pelvis: No lytic or blastic lesions.
IMPRESSION:
1. Stable pancreatic head mass, slightly increased in size.
2. No new intra-abdominal or pelvic pathology.
Additional Notes:
- Follow-up MRI recommended for detailed evaluation of the pancreatic mass.
- Continue with clinical follow-up and routine labs.
