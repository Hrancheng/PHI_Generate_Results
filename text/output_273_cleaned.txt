ABDOMINAL ULTRASOUND WITH DOPPLER 8/5/2022 CLINICAL INDICATION: Suspected liver cirrhosis and portal hypertension. HISTORY: Chronic alcohol use. FINDINGS: The liver is enlarged measuring 18 cm in span and demonstrates coarse echotexture suggestive of cirrhosis. The main portal vein is patent but dilated, measuring 1.5 cm. There is evidence of mild ascites. The spleen is enlarged measuring 14 cm in length. There are no signs of focal hepatic lesions. The common bile duct and gallbladder are unremarkable. Kidneys are normal in size and echo pattern without evidence of hydronephrosis. IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. Findings are consistent with liver cirrhosis likely due to chronic alcohol use. 2. Portal hypertension as evidenced by splenomegaly and dilated portal vein. 3. Mild ascites. Further evaluation with laboratory tests and possible endoscopic examination is recommended.
