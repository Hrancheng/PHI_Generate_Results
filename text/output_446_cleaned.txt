Example 21:
ECHOCARDIOGRAPHY 10/15/2020 10:00 AM INDICATION: 58-year-old male with history of hypertension, presenting with dyspnea on exertion. COMPARISON: Echocardiogram dated 11/20/2019. TECHNIQUE: Transthoracic echocardiography with Doppler and color flow imaging performed using the Cedar Sinai Cardiac Imaging standard protocol. FINDINGS: The left ventricle is normal in size with an estimated ejection fraction of 55%. There is no evidence of segmental wall motion abnormalities. The left atrium is mildly dilated. Mitral valve leaflets are mildly thickened without significant mitral stenosis or regurgitation. Tricuspid and aortic valves are normal. Mild pulmonary hypertension is noted with pulmonary artery systolic pressure estimated at 35 mmHg. The right ventricle and right atrium are normal in size and function. No pericardial effusion or clots are seen. IMPRESSION: 1. Normal left ventricular systolic function. 2. Mild left atrial enlargement. 3. Mild pulmonary hypertension.
