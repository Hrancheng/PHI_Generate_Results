MRI BRAIN WITH AND WITHOUT CONTRAST, 11/14/2022 CLINICAL INDICATION: 63-year-old male with new onset seizures and memory disturbances. Comparison: MRI of the brain dated 10/10/2022, 8/8/2022. TECHNIQUE: 1. MRI performed on a 3 Tesla unit using the General Hospital comprehensive brain protocol. Images were obtained before and after the administration of 15 mL of Gadobutrol intravenous contrast. FINDINGS: There is a new 2.0 x 2.3 cm enhancing lesion in the left temporal lobe not present on the previous examination. This lesion shows surrounding vasogenic edema and mass effect resulting in mild displacement of the adjacent structures. The hippocampus and adjacent white matter otherwise appear unremarkable. No evidence of acute hemorrhage or acute infarction. The ventricles and sulci are otherwise normal in size and configuration. No midline shift. The visualized portions of the posterior fossa are without abnormality. IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. New left temporal lobe lesion as described, concerning for primary central nervous system neoplasm; recommend further evaluation with biopsy for histopathology and tumor characterization. 2. No evidence of acute hemorrhage or infarction.
