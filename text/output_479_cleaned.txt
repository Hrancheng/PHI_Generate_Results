Example 21:
CT Head Without Contrast: 
DATE: September 12, 2023
HISTORY: Sudden onset of severe headache and vision changes. 
FINDINGS: The study was performed without contrast enhancement. There is no evidence of intracranial hemorrhage or acute territorial infarction. Ventricular size and sulcal pattern are within normal limits for patient's age. There is no midline shift or mass effect. The visualized portions of the paranasal sinuses are clear. The mastoid air cells are well aerated. No significant abnormalities are detected in the visualized cranio-cervical junction, and no significant bony abnormalities are identified.
IMPRESSION: 
1. No acute intracranial pathology.
2. Stable appearances of cerebral anatomy.
3. Recommend clinical correlation for persistent symptoms and consider follow-up MRI with contrast if clinically indicated.
