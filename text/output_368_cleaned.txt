CT ABDOMEN AND PELVIS WITH CONTRAST 11/15/2022 CLINICAL INDICATION: 53-year-old male with unexplained weight loss and intermittent abdominal pain. COMPARISON: Prior abdominal CT from 05/20/2021. TECHNIQUE: Multi-phasic CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis was performed utilizing oral and IV contrast. FINDINGS: The liver is not enlarged with normal attenuation without any focal lesions. The gallbladder is surgically absent. The pancreas is unremarkable without any masses or ductal dilatation. Kidneys are normal in size and shape with no evidence of stones or hydronephrosis. The adrenal glands are normal. There is a small amount of free fluid in the pelvis. The spleen is slightly enlarged measuring 13 cm but homogeneous. The abdominal aorta is normal in caliber with no aneurysmal dilation. IMPRESSION: No significant change from prior study on 05/20/2021, mild splenomegaly noted, likely incidental. Further imaging follow-up is recommended in one year unless symptoms exacerbate.
