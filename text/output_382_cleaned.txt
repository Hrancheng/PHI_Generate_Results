MR ABDOMEN WITH AND WITHOUT CONTRAST, 10/23/2022 10:45 AM INDICATION: 32-year-old male with a history of chronic pancreatitis, assessing for potential cysts. COMPARISON: MR Abdomen dated 09/15/2021. TECHNIQUE: MRI of the abdomen was performed on a 3.0 Tesla scanner using a pancreas protocol which includes MRCP sequences. Images were obtained pre and post the administration of 15 mL of Gadovist intravenous contrast. FINDINGS: Pancreas shows heterogeneous enhancement indicative of chronic pancreatitis. No obvious mass lesions or pancreatic ductal dilation. Several small cysts are noted within the pancreas, largest measuring approximately 1.2 x 1.1 cm. Liver, spleen, gallbladder, adrenal glands, kidneys, and bowel loops appear normal. No free fluid or lymphadenopathy is evident. IMPRESSION: IMPRESSION: 1. Findings consistent with chronic pancreatitis with the presence of several pancreatic cysts. No significant interval change from prior exam. 2. No evidence of malignancy or acute inflammatory changes.
