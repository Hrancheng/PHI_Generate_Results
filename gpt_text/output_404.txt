Example 21:
PROCEDURE: CT ABDOMEN AND PELVIS WITH AND WITHOUT CONTRAST [B-DATE]7/17/2022[O]  
CLINICAL INDICATION: Evaluation for abdominal pain and suspected pancreatitis. COMPARISON: Ultrasound [B-DATE]1/15/2022[O].  
TECHNIQUE: Multidetector CT scan performed with oral and IV contrast.  
FINDINGS:  
1. The pancreas is enlarged with peripancreatic fat stranding consistent with acute pancreatitis.  
2. No gallstones or biliary dilatation.  
3. Liver, spleen, adrenal glands, and kidneys are normal in appearance.  
4. No evidence of abdominal or pelvic masses.  
5. Trace free fluid in the pelvis is likely reactive.  
IMPRESSION:  
1. CT findings supportive of acute pancreatitis.  
2. No complications such as necrosis or abscess formation.  
3. Recommend clinical correlation and follow-up labs including lipase and amylase levels.
